i.thesis

The contemporary spiritual seeker is being failed in two opposite directions — by Western apps that have the aesthetic and not the depth, and by Indian apps that have the depth and not the soul.

Devi occupies the empty quadrant: a premium daily companion built on a sidereal engine, served with the polish of the best Western lifestyle apps — by the people whose tradition this is.

ii.the problem

The market is broken in opposite ways.

  • Western apps — Co-Star, Chani — have aesthetic and a daily loop, but lean entirely on tropical zodiac. Psychologically resonant; cosmologically shallow.
  • Indian apps — AstroSage, AstroTalk — have computational depth and Vedic authenticity, but trap users in transactional pay-per-minute consultations wrapped in cluttered UIs.
  • Meditation apps — Calm, Headspace — are stagnating. Subscriber declines, content fatigue, no ritual structure beyond generic breathwork.
  • No app bridges Western and Vedic. Users are forced to pick a tradition or run two apps in parallel.

v.the wedge

The diaspora arbitrage is real, large, and currently unserved.

Diaspora users pay in USD/GBP. Domestic India pays in rupees at one-tenth the rate. AstroTalk's own data shows 47% of its $140M ARR comes from US/UK/Canada.

Diaspora seekers want their tradition, find domestic apps unbearable, and find Western apps shallow. No one is serving this exact need.

i.two skies, one self

Your tropical chart shows the personality you wear.
Your sidereal chart shows the soul beneath it.

No app on the market lets you see both lenses simultaneously, let alone construct a personal archetype from elements of each.

We translate the deepest dispute in modern astrology into the deepest feature in modern astrology — not by picking a winner, but by honoring both. Two lenses on one sky.

ii.the daily ritual

Not a horoscope to read. A ritual to do.

The Living Mandala — a 1-to-3 minute interactive sacred-geometry practice anchored to your local sunrise, your nakshatra of the day, and the time windows that have organized Hindu spiritual life for millennia.

It is the first thing you do when you wake. The last thing before bed. Calm has a meditation library; Devi has an anchor.

iii.sacred timing

The hours your grandmother knew about.

Brahma Muhurta. Abhijit Muhurta.
Rahu Kalam. Yama Gandam. Gulika Kalam.

Computed to the second for your exact location. Surfaced as gentle daily anchors — not anxiety triggers. The same calculations professional Vedic astrologers use, served with the typography and restraint of a printed almanac.

iv.synastry, not surveillance

Compatibility built to illuminate, not weaponize.

Vedic Ashtakoot and Kuta scoring — eight dimensions of granular astrological alignment. Specific, empathetic, and deeply shareable.

"Your communication styles clash —
but your long-term spiritual missions align."

Co-Star's compatibility went viral by translating astrology into bite-sized verdicts. We do the same — but with eight times the depth and none of the cynicism.

v.where to be

Pick what your soul is doing this season.

Existing astrocartography tools require a textbook. Devi reverses it — pick an intent (deepening practice, processing grief, expanding, recovering from burnout) and the app shows you the geographic zones where your chart resonates.

For travelers, sojourners, the geographically curious — and the diaspora seeker quietly asking where they belong.
